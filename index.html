St. John's University

Queens, NY

Before I transferred to Penn, I spent my freshman year at St. John's University (SJU). St. John's University is a private Catholic university founded in 1870 by the Vincentian Fathers, with its main campus in the Queens, New York. Rooted in Vincentian values of social justice, charity, and service, the university also maintains campuses in Manhattan and international locations in Rome, Paris, and Limerick, Ireland. It is recognized as one of the nation's most diverse higher education institutions, with strong ties to New York City's professional and cultural landscape.

This map highlights the D'Angelo Center, or "DAC", which is the main student life center on campus. Also shown in the red circle is the campus bookstore and Dunkin' Donuts coffee cafe
